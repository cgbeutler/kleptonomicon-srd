02 Why Play Kleptonomicon

There are many TTRPGs out there. Any veteran of these games can tell you it is better to pick the game that fits how you want to play than to try to force a game to be what it is not.

So what does The Kleptonomicon do best and what does it not do very well?

What The Kleptonomicon does best is social intrigue, stealth, heist, and lighter combat focused campaigns. The Kleptonomicon gives each Player unique Social Skills and Social Abilities, allowing everyone to participate in and manipulate social situations. Every player will also have a unique set of Covert Skills and Abilities. Players will need to learn to embrace teamwork, much like in a heist movie, in order to uncover and navigate the magical and non-magical security systems of the worlds powerful and wealthy.

So, what The Kleptonomicon not do well? If you are looking for hyper-realism or a game that simulates how things would work more in real life, you may want to try games like 'Pathfinder' instead. The Kleptonomicon assumes that players can use logic and good storytelling principles to fill in the gaps instead of making rules for more niche situations. On the flip side, The Kleptonomicon is not fully a narrative-first game and has turn-based combat and rules for things like taking fall damage and grappling with your opponent. If you would like a game more narrative than what is provided in this book, check out 'Monster of the Week' or the other 'Powered by the Apocalypse' games.

A More Detailed Look

If you are familiar with many other TTRPGs and would like even more detail on how The Kleptonomicon compares to other games, below is a more detailed breakdown.

The Kleptonomicon is heavily inspired by both simulation TTRPGs (like Dungeons and Dragons 5th Edition) and storytelling TTRPGs (like Dungeon World) and tries to strike a mid-point between the two styles. The game is more simulation light than the older editions of 'Dungeons and Dragons', introducing many ideologies from the more storytelling-heavy TTRPGs you know and love.

Below are some of the main differences found in The Kleptonomicon. You should weight these motivations to determine if the balance struck by The Kleptonomicon matches what you are looking for for your next game:

  • There are three Social Stats, not one.
    • Many TTRPGs have a single social stat. The Kleptonomicon instead has three Social Stats that are siloed off from the rest of your stats. The Social Stats are rolled separately from the the other stats and have little effect on your choice of Class, ensuring that each character has some social strengths.
    • Why? TTRPGs with only one social stat often lead one or two players being given the role of the 'face' of the group. This can lead to whole scenes or episodes where some players feel they have to sit out, as joining in would likely only lead to failure. The lop-sided nature of a single social stat makes social intrigue campaigns difficult to balance in terms of play-time. The Kleptonomicon seeks to fix this by making the social side of the game into a system all its own. This also means that players have more freedom as to which social roles they wish to fill. Depending on what Social Abilities they chooses, Players can even design the more passive, knowledge-heavy socials roles, if they prefer to do less role-playing, reminiscent of using charisma as your dump stat.
  • There are three Covert Skills spread out across the core stats.
    • Many games clump the Covert Skills under a single stat. The Kleptonomicon instead divides stealth into three skills, one for each Exploration Stat. This ensures that all players have different strengths when undergoing covert operations.
    • Why? The motivation here is similar to those for the three social stats. Games with only one stealth stat can lead to whole scenes and episodes where only one or two players can shine while the others have to sit aside and wait, or risk tanking everything. The Kleptonomicon is a stealth-first system that works to make it easy to balance a heist or stealth-heavy campaign. The three Covert Skills combined with simple stealth rules and a system of security-based spells means heist campaigns are easier to balance and more fun to play.
  • All spellcasting using a single 'Tuning' stat.
    • All spellcasting has been placed under a single stat called "Tuning". Tuning has more to do with your ability to resonate with magic and less to do with how you got there. There is no 'Intelligence' or 'Wisdom' stat.
    • Why? Spellcasting and taking spells through different avenues is more simple. Also, as a small benefit, terms like 'Intelligence' and 'Wisdom' are no longer there to pigeonhole certain characters based on their Class. In other words, you can now play as a dumb wizard or a brilliant barbarian without any stat to hold you back. That said, there are still Knowledge Skills that you may need to roll to recall more specific areas of knowledge.
  • There is no basic, starting armor or weapons.
    • Your character's basic armor and weapons are treated as flavor, and nothing more. You can skip searching a large list of items thar are barely different from one-another and focus on the vibe you want for your character. That said, you can still become Unarmed, gain magic items, and pick up Abilities that change the reach and effects of your Basic Attacks, but those tweaks happen later or are opt-in.
    • Why? During playtesting, we slowly came to realize that basic weapons and armor were... well... not actually adding much to the game. Having lists of equipment to sort through just lead to a mess of balancing and min-maxing that didn't actually add to the fun of playing the game. We experimented with just providing everyone with a damage and armor value based on your Archetype choice, and it was a hit. No more homework to figure out what is allowed or works best for your character. That homework is, essentially, done for you. The specifics about your exact loadout are left up to your imagination. Do you want to carry a giant broom to hit people with? Go right ahead! You don't even have to figure out what item that is closest to! That said, if you want your weapons to have capabilities that go beyond what the Basic Attacks allow, you will need to spend Feature Points to pick up Features that add those capabilities.
  • Wealth Scores replace Money. Players can improvise loot themselves and shop on their own.
    • Each character is given a unique mix of Wealth Skills that represent a niche of financial acuity. The Specialists, for example, are masters of #Grey_Wealth, meaning they know how to find illicit goods and back-room products. The Casters get more #Obscure_Wealth, allowing them to find and buying strange ingredients, wands, crystal balls, and so on. And, lastly, the Fighters are more well known in common merchant circles, granting them more #General_Wealth to find everyday items, adventuring supplies, armors, and weapons. No matter what your Wealth Skills are, you can use them to improvise items not explicitly found in your inventory (implying that you found it somewhere along the way.)
    • Why? Math and bookkeeping is fun and all, but most players seem to shy away from it. Many GMs seem to lose interest in figuring out and handing out small rewards by the end of a game. Players sometimes have to beg for a "shopping episode" just to get their gear up to the specs they probably should have been at anyway. The Kleptonomicon provides a simplified system that allows players to shop on their own time while providing simple, generic rewards that the GM can hand out. Players can also improvise items at any time using their wealth. Need a hairbrush for the plan to work? Simply spend some of your wealth to retroactively imply that you picked one up in the last room! (But beware: If you want a strange or rare item, you may have to roll for it...)

If you are still here after all of that, then allow me to extend a warm welcome from The Kleptonomicon. Unless, of course, you are here to steal from The Kleptonomicon, then may you be torn limb-from-limb, your skin flayed and your bones boiled with the heat of in a thousand suns! Only The Kleptonomicon is allowed to steal! If you're just "borrowing" on the other hand... well then I would like to once again extend you a warm welcome from The Kleptonomicon (and ask you if I could join your campaign.)
