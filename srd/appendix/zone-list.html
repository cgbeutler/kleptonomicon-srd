Acid Zone

icon: LiBoxSelect
iconColor: orange

summary

When a creature starts their turn touching an Acid Zone or when they touch an Acid Zone for the first time on their turn, the creature loses 1 Shield. If the creature's shield was at 0, they must instead Pass a Grit Save or gain 1 stack of Bloodied for each stack of Acid Zone.

Airless Zone

icon: LiBoxSelect
iconColor: orange
tags:
  - Core

summary

When a creature starts their turn touching an Airless Zone or when they touch a Airless Zone for the first time on their turn, the creature experiences the following effects:

  • The creature gains 1 stack of Silenced for each stack of Airless Zone.
  • If the creature would gain stacks of Silenced beyond 2 stacks, they must Pass a Grit Save or gain a stack of Choked. The Choked Condition becomes a Fleeting Condition upon leaving the Airless Zone.

Before a creature enters this Zone, they can choose to hold their breath. Doing so has the following effect:

  • The creature gains two stacks of Silenced as a Persistent Condition. It becomes a Fleeting Condition again when they next breathe air.
  • The creature can automatically succeed the Saves for this Zone while holding their breath.

A creature can only hold their breath for a number of Turns equal to 5 plus their #athleticism_skill. Speaking or casting spells that require vocal components count as an end to holding your breath.

Creatures that do not need to breathe are not affected by an Airless Zone.

Dark Zone

icon: LiBoxSelect
iconColor: orange
tags:
  - Core

Any object or creature within a Dark Zone is affected. The affected has the following effects for each stack of Dark Zone:

  • The affected gains one stack of the #vulnerable_condition.
  • The affected gains one Disadvantage on Tests when targeting something other than #self.
  • The affected gains one Advantage on Covert Skill Tests against being seen.
  • Any Tests that target the affected gain one Disadvantage.

Cancelations:

  • A Dim Light within 5u can cancel out one stack of Dark Zone.
  • A Bright Light can cancel out two stacks of Dark Zones within 10u and one stack of a Dark Zone within 5u.

Fire Zone

icon: LiBoxSelect
iconColor: orange
tags:
  - Core

When a creature starts their turn touching a Fire Zone or when they touch a Fire Zone for the first time on their turn, the creature must Pass an Grit Save or take Fluid/Fire damage equal to the number of Fire Zone stacks.

A Fire Zone can spread on its own to an adjacent space if a sufficient fuel source exists in that location. Stacks can also be added to an existing Fire Zone if a fuel source is sufficiently flammable. The rate of spreading and intensifying is determined by the GM and may be exacerbated by a Wind Zone. More intense fires will generally burn for less time.

A Fire Zone counts as a #dim_light. At 2 stacks or more, a Fire Zone counts as a #bright_light.

Cancelations:

  • One stack of Fire Zone can be cleared per Round by dousing it with water.

Fog Zone

icon: LiBoxSelect
iconColor: orange
tags:
  - Core

Any object or creature within a Fog Zone is affected. The affected has the following effects for each stack of Fog Zone:

  • The affected gains one stack of the #vulnerable_condition.
  • The affected gains one Disadvantage on Tests when targeting something outside Swinging Distance.
  • The affected gains one Advantage on Covert Skill Tests against being seen.
  • Any Tests that target the affected gain one Disadvantage if they come from further away than Swinging Distance.

Any creatures outside of the Fog Zone can use the Fog Zone as Cover. This grants them a +2 #secondary_defense and one Advantage on Covert Skill Tests against being seen.

Cancelations:

  • One stack of Wind Zone cancels out one stack of Fog Zone.

Loud Zone

icon: LiBoxSelect
iconColor: orange
tags:
  - Core

Any object or creature within a Loud Zone is affected. The affected has the following effects for each stack of Loud Zone:

  • The affected creature gains an Advantage on Covert Skill Tests against being heard.
  • All Covert Skill Tests to prevent being heard by the affected creature have an Advantage.

Additionally, all creatures in True Shooting Distance can hear the Loud Zone and follow its sound.

At two stacks, any creature within the Loud Zone that has hearing takes 1 Fluid/Blunt Damage unless they plug their ears. Doing so gives them one stack of Restrained and Deafened.

Toxic Zone

icon: LiBoxSelect
iconColor: orange
tags:
  - Core

| A cloud of toxic gas, spores, or other breathable corrosive particles.

When a creature starts their turn touching a Toxic Zone or when they touch a Toxic Zone for the first time on their turn, they must roll a Grit Save. A Low Score grants the creature 1 stack of Bloodied for each stack of this Toxic Zone.

Before a creature enters this Zone, they can choose to hold their breath. Doing so has the following effect:

  • The creature gains two stacks of Silenced as a Persistent Condition that becomes a Fleeting Condition when they next breathe air.
  • The creature can automatically succeed the Saves for this Zone while holding their breath.

A creature can only hold their breath for a number of Turns equal to 5 plus their #athleticism_skill. Creatures that do not need to breathe are not affected by a Toxic Zone.

Cancelations:

  • One stack of ^Wind Zone can cancels out one stack of Toxic Zone.

Wind Zone

icon: LiBoxSelect
iconColor: orange
tags:
  - Core

Each 1u cube of a Wind Zone has a direction in which it blows.

Creatures that start their turn entirely within the Wind Zone or that entirely enter the Wind Zone on their turn must Pass a Grit Save or be pushed in the direction the wind is blowing a number of units equal to the number of stacks of Wind Zone.

The wind is not strong enough to causes harm or to make a non-airborne creature airborne. Airborne creatures and Tiny or smaller creatures automatically Fail the Save. Pushing an airborne creature doesn't end flying or levitating. Creatures with a Climbing Speed automatically Pass the Test if they are not airborne.

Any object weighing less than or equal to 5 pounds times the number of Wind Zone Stacks automatically fails the Save, provided it is not being carried or held. If the object is not airborne, the wind cannot make it airborne.

Cancelations:

  • Co-located stacks of Wind Zone that blow in opposite directions cancel each-other out at a rate of one-to-one.

Untether Zone

icon: LiBoxSelect
iconColor: orange

While touching an Untethering Zone, a creature's Untethered Condition becomes a Persistent Condition.

When a creature starts their turn touching an Untethering Zone or when they touch an Untethering Zone for the first time on their turn, the creature must make a Focus Save. A Low Score inflicts one stack of Untethered for each stack of Untethering Zone and, if the creature now has 2 stacks of Untethered, they must roll a d6, potentially gaining a random effect:

  • [0!] The creature takes 1 #true_damage.
  • [1] The creature gains one stack of Polyformed, choosing their own form from the following: Animated Chair, Butterfly, Rabbit, Sheep.
  • [2] The creature shrinks until the start of their next turn, as if under the effects of the Enlarge Spell.
  • [3] The creature forgets their current known Languages and speaks a distant, unknown language.
  • [4] The creature's weapons and equipment make a #noisy squeaking sound when used.
  • [5] The creature changes lineage, but only visually.
  • [6] The creature's clothes change to a different color.
  • [7] The creature suddenly knows a small secret about the world or another party member.
  • [8] The creature grows larger until the start of their next turn, as if under the effects of the Enlarge Spell.
  • [9] The creature gains one stack of Polyformed, choosing their own form from the following:
  • [10!] The creature heals 1 Health.

When the creator of this Zone is affected by it, they can roll two effect d6s and choose whichever effect they want.
